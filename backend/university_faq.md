## Course Registration
Course registration opens at the start of each semester and runs for two weeks.
Students register online through the Student Information System (SIS) using their
registration number and password. You must clear any outstanding fees before the
system will allow registration. Late registration is permitted in the third week
but attracts a late-registration penalty fee. Add-and-drop of courses is allowed
during the first two weeks only.

## Examination Rules
Students must register for a course to sit its examination. A minimum of 75%
class attendance is required to be eligible for the final examination. Bring your
student identity card to every exam; entry without it is not allowed. Mobile
phones and unauthorised materials are prohibited in the examination hall.
Examination results are published through the SIS portal. A student who misses an
exam for a valid medical reason may apply for a special (supplementary) exam
within seven days, attaching a medical report.

## Library Services
The main library is open Monday to Friday from 8:00 AM to 10:00 PM and on
weekends from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Undergraduate students may borrow up to five
books for two weeks; postgraduates may borrow up to ten books for one month.
Overdue books attract a daily fine per book. The library provides access to
e-journals and past papers through the online catalogue, which you log into with
your student account. Quiet study rooms can be booked at the front desk.

## ICT Support
ICT support handles student email, Wi-Fi access, the learning management system,
and account password resets. To reset a forgotten password, visit the ICT help
desk with your student ID or use the self-service reset link on the portal. Every
student gets a university email account and free campus Wi-Fi using their student
credentials. Report broken lab computers or network problems by raising a ticket
on the ICT help desk portal. The help desk is open on weekdays from 8:00 AM to
5:00 PM.

## Hostel Application
On-campus hostel accommodation is limited and allocated on a first-come,
first-served basis with priority for first-year and final-year students. Apply
online through the accommodation module on the SIS during the application window,
which opens before each academic year. Payment of the hostel fee confirms your
allocation; an unpaid allocation is released after the deadline. Students with
medical needs can request priority placement by attaching supporting documents.

## Fee Payment
Tuition and other fees are paid through the bank using the control number
generated on the SIS, or via the approved mobile-money channels. Always generate
a fresh control number for each payment and keep the receipt. Fees must be paid
before registration and before sitting examinations. Students facing financial
hardship can apply for an instalment plan at the bursar's office before the fee
deadline. Loan-board (HESLB) disbursements are reflected on the student account
once released.

## Academic Calendar
The academic year has two semesters, each about fifteen weeks of teaching
followed by an examination period. Key dates - semester start, registration
window, mid-semester break, examinations, and results release - are published on
the university website and the SIS noticeboard. Public holidays are observed as
gazetted. Always check the official academic calendar for the current year, as
dates may shift.

## Student Conduct
All students must follow the university code of conduct. Examination malpractice
(cheating, impersonation, or bringing unauthorised materials) is a serious
offence and can lead to suspension or expulsion. Plagiarism in coursework is
penalised. Disciplinary cases are heard by the students' disciplinary committee,
and students have the right to be heard and to appeal. Respect for staff, fellow
students, and university property is expected at all times.
